The worst reason to go multicloud

Preventing lock-in. That’s it, that’s the worst reason to go multicloud. Why? You can’t do it, says Richard Seroter. Everything is lock-in.

Instead, the teams that are for-real doing multicloud seem to fall into at least one of five common adoption patterns:

1. Cap and grow. Freeze your initial cloud in place, and invest primarily in the next cloud. This is happening more often than before.
2. Extend the foundation. Treat the current cloud as the anchor, and “stretch” it to your secondary clouds. It’s a reasonable strategy that helps you keep existing skills in place.
3. Parallel paths. Intentionally (or unintentionally) grow each cloud independently. This is what often happens in organizations with very independent departments. Long-term, this isn’t the smartest strategy as you’ll end up with significant duplication and complexity.
4. Selective services. Choose a small number of best-of-breed services from a secondary cloud, but keep growing your footprint in your primary cloud. This is often how people begin their multicloud journey.
5. Isolation for regional/compliance needs. Add and grow your secondary cloud in specific locations to satisfy the needs of that region.
